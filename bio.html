About Me

I was a Marketing Manager for a small consulting firm when my boss gave me the assignment to write a sales proposal, which I had never done but thoroughly enjoyed. When the project was complete, my boss informed me that I was "...a really good Technical Writer." I expressed my gratitude for the compliment and quickly launched an investigation into this Technical Writer thing. As it turns out, a person can actually make a living at it. And so I have.

I walked into my first contract job as a Technical Writer in March of 2000 with virtually no experience or training. I soon discovered I had an aptitude for learning software programs well enough to write online help. Fortunately, I was able to teach myself HTML in time to deliver the project.

Since then I've held both salary and contract positions with small, medium, and large organizations. As technology has grown, I've continuously developed my skills with the tools and coding languages I use in producing a variety of materials required by each of these organizations.

In my downtime, well, I write. Children's stories, wiki articles, and (because I'm a gamer) walkthroughs are my favorites. I also play the guitar from time to time, and the occasional do-it-yourself home improvement project.
